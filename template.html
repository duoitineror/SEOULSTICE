Transportation

  • Subway: Reliable, easy to navigate. Use the T-money card for convenience.
  • KTX: High-speed train connecting major cities.
  • T-money Card: Prepaid card for transit, taxis, and convenience stores.

Currency & Budget Tips

  • Currency: South Korean Won (KRW).
  • Budget: Affordable to mid-range; luxury options available. Street food is a great budget-friendly option.
  • ATMs: Widely available. Credit cards are accepted in most places.

Safety & Etiquette

  • South Korea is very safe. Just be cautious in crowded areas.
  • Etiquette: Respect for elders and formal behavior is important in both public and private settings.

Travel Styles

  • Solo Travel: Safe and easy, with a lot to explore.
  • Family: Kid-friendly attractions and activities.
  • Backpacking: Budget-friendly accommodations and vibrant social scenes.
  • Luxury: High-end hotels, spas, and fine dining experiences.

Suggested Itineraries

3-Day Itinerary
  • Day 1: Explore Seoul (Gyeongbokgung Palace, Bukchon Hanok Village, N Seoul Tower).
  • Day 2: Shopping and sightseeing in Myeongdong, Namsan Park, and Dongdaemun.
  • Day 3: Day trip to DMZ or Nami Island for a mix of history and nature.
7-Day Itinerary
  • Days 1-2: Explore Seoul (Palaces, markets, Gangnam district).
  • Day 3: Day trip to DMZ or Incheon.
  • Day 4-5: Visit Jeonju (Korean food, Hanok Village).
  • Day 6-7: Busan ‚Äì Beaches, Gamcheon Culture Village, shopping.
2-Week Itinerary
  • Days 1-3: Explore Seoul (historic sites, shopping districts).
  • Day 4-5: Visit Jeonju (Korean food, traditional village).
  • Day 6-7: Gyeongju (temples, historical sites).
  • Day 8-10: Busan (beaches, hiking, culture).
  • Day 11-13: Jeju Island (nature, hiking, beaches).
  • Day 14: Return to Seoul for last-minute shopping and relaxation.
