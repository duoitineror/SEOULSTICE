Food & Drinks

Discover Korea’s flavors — from spicy street snacks to hearty traditional meals.

🍲 Food & Drinks

Popular Street Foods

Tteokbokki (떡볶이) — Spicy-sweet stir-fried rice cakes in gochujang sauce, often with fish cakes and eggs.

Soondae (순대) — Korean blood sausage stuffed with glass noodles, rice, and seasoning.

Dakkochi (닭꼬치) — Grilled chicken skewers glazed with a sweet-spicy sauce.

Gyeran-ppang (계란빵) — Fluffy egg bread with a whole egg inside.

Eomuk / Odeng (어묵 / 오뎅) — Fish cakes served in warm broth.

Gimbap (김밥) — Rolled seaweed rice with assorted fillings.

Bungeoppang (붕어빵) — Fish-shaped pastry with sweet red bean or custard filling.

Hotteok (호떡) — Sweet pancake filled with brown sugar, cinnamon, and nuts.

Corn Dogs (감자핫도그) — Korean corn dogs coated with potato bits and sugar.

So-tteok-so-tteok (소떡소떡) — Skewered rice cakes and sausages brushed with sauce.

Ojingeo Twigim (오징어 튀김) — Deep-fried squid, crispy and savory.

Traditional Dishes

Bibimbap (비빔밥) — Rice topped with vegetables, beef, egg, and gochujang; mixed before eating.

Bulgogi (불고기) — Sweet-savory marinated beef grilled to perfection.

Japchae (잡채) — Glass noodles stir-fried with vegetables and light soy seasoning.

Kimchi-jjigae (김치찌개) — Hearty kimchi stew with tofu and pork.

Doenjang-jjigae (된장찌개) — Soybean paste stew rich in umami flavor.

Samgyetang (삼계탕) — Ginseng chicken soup for health and energy.

Gukbap (국밥) — Soup served with rice, a comforting Korean staple.

Nakji-bokkeum (낙지볶음) — Spicy stir-fried octopus with vegetables.

Drinks

Soju — Korea’s famous clear spirit, smooth and slightly sweet.

Makgeolli — Traditional milky rice wine with a tangy sweetness.

Beer (Maekju) — Commonly enjoyed with Korean fried chicken (“chimaek”).

Tea — Traditional teas like green tea (nokcha) and barley tea (boricha) are popular and historic.
