Travel Tips

Tips to help make your experience easier and more enjoyable! Following these guarantees less hassle and an amazing journey.

Essentials

🇰🇷 Language Basics

  • Annyeong (안녕) – Hello/Goodbye (informal)
  • Kamsahamnida (감사합니다) – Thank you (formal)
  • Juseyo (주세요) – Please (request)
  • Mianhamnida (미안합니다) – Sorry (formal)

🚉 Transportation

  • Subway: Reliable, easy to navigate. Use the T-money card for convenience.
  • KTX: High-speed train connecting major cities.
  • T-money Card: Prepaid card for transit, taxis, and convenience stores.

₩ Currency & Budget Tips

  • Currency: South Korean Won (KRW).
  • Budget: Affordable to mid-range; luxury options available. Street food is a great budget-friendly option.
  • ATMs: Widely available. Credit cards are accepted in most places.

👷🏻‍♂️ Safety & Etiquette

  • South Korea is very safe. Just be cautious in crowded areas.
  • Etiquette: Respect for elders and formal behavior is important in both public and private settings.

🛫 Travel Styles

  • Solo Travel: Safe and easy, with a lot to explore.
  • Family: Kid-friendly attractions and activities.
  • Backpacking: Budget-friendly accommodations and vibrant social scenes.
  • Luxury: High-end hotels, spas, and fine dining experiences.

Do's and Don'ts in Korean Culture

Do’s:

  • Bow slightly when greeting or thanking someone (a nod is fine for casual)
  • Use both hands when giving/receiving items (money, gifts, business cards)
  • Remove shoes before entering homes and some traditional restaurants.
  • Address people by their title + surname (ex. “Manager Kim”), unless invited to use first names.
  • Respect elders — let them eat first, pour drinks for them, and don’t start eating before they do.

Don’ts:

  • Don’t expect personal space to be the same as in Western countries—crowded areas are normal.
  • Don’t blow your nose loudly in public (seen as rude).
  • Don’t write someone’s name in red ink (traditionally linked with death).
  • Avoid direct confrontation or loud arguments in public.
  • Don’t interrupt people while they speak

Common Gestures & Meanings

  • Bowing → Respect, greeting, or thanks.
  • V sign (peace sign) → Common in photos, means “cute” or “happy,” not victory.
  • Palm up, fingers together motion → Means “come here” (different from Western palm-down).
  • X shape with arms → Strong “No” or rejection.
  • Finger heart → Popular sign of love/affection.
  • Waving palm facing down → “Come here” (facing up looks unnatural).
