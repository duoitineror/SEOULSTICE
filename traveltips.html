Travel Tips

Tips to help make your experience easier and more enjoyable! Following these guarantees less hassle and an amazing journey.

Do’s & Don’ts in Korean Culture

Do’s:

  • Bow slightly when greeting or thanking someone (a nod is fine for casual)
  • Use both hands when giving/receiving items (money, gifts, business cards)
  • Remove shoes before entering homes and some traditional restaurants.
  • Address people by their title + surname (ex. “Manager Kim”), unless invited to use first names.
  • Respect elders — let them eat first, pour drinks for them, and don’t start eating before they do.

Don’ts:

  • Don’t expect personal space to be the same as in Western countries—crowded areas are normal.
  • Don’t blow your nose loudly in public (seen as rude).
  • Don’t write someone’s name in red ink (traditionally linked with death).
  • Avoid direct confrontation or loud arguments in public.
  • Don’t interrupt people while they speak
Common Gestures & Meanings Bowing → Respect, greeting, or thanks. V sign (peace sign) → Common in photos, means “cute” or “happy,” not victory. Palm up, fingers together motion → Means “come here” (different from Western palm-down). X shape with arms → Strong “No” or rejection. Finger heart → Popular sign of love/affection. Waving palm facing down → “Come here” (facing up looks unnatural).
