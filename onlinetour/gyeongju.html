The Ancient Capital

Known as “the museum without walls,” Gyeongju immerses you in the glory of the Silla Dynasty.

  • Major Places

Bulguksa Temple

A UNESCO treasure, Bulguksa is a masterpiece of Buddhist art and architecture. Its pagodas and bridges whisper stories of devotion and beauty that have lasted more than a millennium.

Anapji Pond

By day it’s a tranquil park, but at night Anapji transforms into a dreamlike scene of shimmering reflections. Once a royal pleasure garden, it’s now one of Korea’s most romantic sights.

Gyochon Traditional Village

From the name itself, Gyochon Village immerses you into the historical traditions of the Korean people, offering well-organized tours and educational experiences.
