Jeju Island, South Korea

Geography & Natural Wonders

  • Located about 130 km off the southern coast of the Korean Peninsula.
  • Home to Hallasan, a dormant volcano and South Korea's highest peak (1,950 m).
  • Features lava tubes, craters, and unique volcanic rock formations.
  • UNESCO World Natural Heritage Site: Hallasan, Geomunoreum Lava Tube System, and Seongsan Ilchulbong Peak.

Climate & Biodiversity

  • Mild subtropical climate with warm summers and mild winters.
  • Rich ecosystems supporting diverse flora and fauna.
  • UNESCO Biosphere Reserve promoting sustainable development.

Cultural Heritage

Tourism & Attractions

  • Hallasan National Park: Hiking trails leading to the summit of Hallasan.
  • Seongsan Ilchulbong (Sunrise Peak): Tuff cone offering panoramic views at sunrise.
  • Manjanggul Lava Tube: One of the longest lava tubes in the world.
  • Jeju Beaches: Hyeopjae and Jungmun beaches ideal for relaxation and water sports.
  • Citrus Orchards: Famous for tangerines, offering fruit tasting and tours.
